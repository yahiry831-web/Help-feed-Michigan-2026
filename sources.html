Sources & Accountability

Providing transparent data through the collaboration of state, national, and community-driven research initiatives.

📍 Michigan Infrastructure

MDHHS Assistance Programs michigan.gov/mdhhs

Primary regulatory body for SNAP (Bridge Cards) and WIC distribution across 83 counties.

Michigan 2-1-1 Dashboard mi211.org

Live data tracking for food-related help requests from residents in real-time.

Gleaners Data Portal gcfb.org

Distribution analytics for Southeast Michigan and the Metro Detroit food rescue network.

🇺🇸 National Analytics

Map the Meal Gap (Feeding America) map.feedingamerica.org

The industry standard for county-level food insecurity statistics and food cost analysis.

USDA Economic Research ers.usda.gov

Federal research on household food security, poverty metrics, and SNAP participation trends.

Census Bureau Pulse Survey census.gov

High-frequency data regarding household food sufficiency and economic impact surveys.

📊 Methodology & Usage

The data displayed throughout Food Access Michigan is aggregated from the aforementioned sources as of 2026. This platform is designed to serve as a centralized resource hub, simplifying complex government and nonprofit data into actionable information for Michigan families. All visualizations and mapping tools are intended for educational and public service use.
